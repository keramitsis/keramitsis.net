Ingredients

  • Greek yogurt (10% fat)
  • Cucumber
  • Fresh Garlic
  • Salt, Pepper -¬†olive¬†oil
  • preferably fresh dill but dried works also (apparently optional)

Well, for the recipe batch I used 300g yoghurt, 3 gloves of garlic and 1 cucumber (they are bigger in Germany so its like 2 japanese cucumbers). No dill at all (had none at hand), according to my mother without is the pure form ü§∑.

For comparison, the party batch had 500g of yoghurt, 3 gloves of garlic, 2 cucumbers, dried dill.

Don't stress about the quantities, eyeball them. In baking you have to be precise, but in cooking we can experiment.

Lets start

Everything on display.

Wash and peel the cucumber. I do it zebra-style to add a bit of crunch. Some people do not like this and remove the hole skin.

Grate the cucumber. Use the largest holes on a box grater or whatever you have at hand.

Add two pinches of salt and spread with your hand. It should look like this ‚òùÔ∏è. Put the cucumber aside.

Take the yoghurt and put it in a bowl or tupeware. Tupeware is probably the better option as we will need to leave it in the fridge at the end. Chop the garlic as finely as possible (I have a quezze trough tool for this). Add to the yoghurt. Stir.

Now would be the time to wash and chop the dill (if you want to add it). Or add half a teaspoon of dried dill. You can add more later if you like. For the fresh dill, add a handful if you have more than you set aside. We will adjust this later.

Stir it all up.

Back to the cucumber. We want to get as much water out as possible. Some people put it all in a cloth and squeeze the water out. I use the hand method of making a ball and squeezing. It should look like this ‚òùÔ∏è.

Now we add our ball of cucumber in the yogurt and stir. You could add 2 tablespoons of olive oil now or skip this and garnish it later on with some olive oil. I left it out. Add some pepper ‚òùÔ∏è. Stir, Stir, Stir. Taste. It probably needs some salt, maybe not. Adjust to your taste. You can add a little more dill if you like.

It is best to leave the yoghurt in the fridge for an hour or even overnight. Wrap it up and put it in the fridge.

You can eat it straight away or eat some and put the rest away. See what the difference is if you let it sit for a while. When you take it out of the fridge, make sure you stir it well.

Enjoy!
