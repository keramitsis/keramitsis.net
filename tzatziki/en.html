Making Tzatziki in Japan

Tzatziki - a Greek classic made with just a few select ingredients. A creamy combination of yogurt and cucumber that goes well with many dishes.

Ingredients and notes

  • Cucumbers
  • Greek yogurt - the original usually contains ~10% fat. In Japan I only found Greek yogurt with about ~5% fat. That's good, but it's still worth comparing when shopping and choosing the higher fat content if possible. A higher fat content makes the tzaziki creamier and tastier.
  • Garlic - use more, less or none at all according to taste.
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Herbs such as parsley, dill, mint - use only one of these or leave out if none are available. I like to use a little parsley. Dill is the classic taste and mint adds a fresh summer flavor.
    • White vinegar or lemon juice - a little acid is recommended, but this ingredient is optional and can be used to taste.

  • 3 cucumbers
  • 320g Greek yoghurt (one pot with 5% fat)
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • a handful of parsley
  • 1 Â½ tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt & Pepper

Wash and peel the cucumbers. I like to leave some skin on the cucumbers to have a little bit of crunch, but the skin can also be removed completely.

Grate the cucumber on a tray or large plate. Use the largest holes on a box grater.

Add a large pinch of salt and spread the grated cucumber. The salt will help to remove water. Set the cucumber aside.

Finely grate the garlic and chop the parsley.

Get a bowl ready to mix everything together.

Now we need to get as much water out of the cucumber as possible.

Traditionally, people like to use some sort of cheesecloth to squeeze out all the water, but I like to use my hands and squeeze the cucumber over the sink (like this).

Put the cucumber in the bowl, add the yogurt, garlic, parsley, Olive Oil and lemon juice.
