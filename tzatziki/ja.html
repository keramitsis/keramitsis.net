Ingredients and notes

  • Cucumber
  • Greek yogurt - typically contains 10% fat. In Japan, I have only found greek yoghurt with about 5% fat. The flavour is good, but it's still worth comparing when shopping and choosing the higher fat content if possible. A higher fat content makes tzaziki creamier.
  • Garlic - use more, less or none according to taste
  • Oliver oil
  • Salt & pepper
  • Herbs such as parsley, dill, mint - you should only use one of these or leave them out altogether. I like to use a little parsley these days, dill is the more classic taste and mint gives a fresher flavour.
  • white vinegar or lemon juice - lately I like to use one of these to enhance the flavour, but the ingredient is optional and can be used to taste.

Wash and peel the cucumbers. I like to leave some skin on the cucumbers, but the skin can also be removed completely.

Grate the cucumber on a tray or big plate. I use the largest holes on a box grater.

Add a large pinch of salt and spread the cucumber evenly with your fingers. The salt will help to remove more water. Leave the cucumber in this state for at least 10 to 15 minutes.
